# Strategic Risks

Long-term risks that the fully-agentic flow might pose to Konflux.


## Threat 1: dependency on AI suppliers for a core pilar

In a fully agentic workflow, we will be outsourcing maintenance of software to AI models, and we will thus be heavily reliant on them as suppliers.

### Why is it dangerous

- If AI suppliers ramp up model prices over time, the financial pressure will negatively impact development and maintenance of Konflux
    - this is the long term tendency, as AI becomes more widely used and more projects become reliant on AI; AI providers are companies, and will seek to maximize profit
- If AI models suffer disruptions, we will be impacted (though this can be mitigated by using several models/providers)
- In the fully-agentic scenario, we are handing over to AI providers a core aspect of Konflux, and thus disempowering the community that used to develop it


## Threat 2: adopting fully-agentic flow will lower human-engineers ability to contribute

In a fully-agentic workflow, human engineers will have less contact with the codebase. Thus, their capability of contributing fixes/changes will be impacted negatively.

### Why is it dangerous

- Imagine that, at some point, human intervention becomes necessary; since human engineers will be less involved with the codebase, their capability of contributing fixes will be jeopardized, and the underlying problem will take longer to be fixed
    - as examples, this could happen if a complex vulnerability, which AI is not able to solve, is found; or if the AI agents suffer some form of drift and code loses quality


## Threat 3: reputational risk in case bug/vulnerability gets released
 
In a fully-agentic workflow, if a bug or vulnerability gets released, and it could have been prevented with human supervision, the project will suffer a great reputational loss.

### Why is it dangerous

- Konflux aims to be a build platform that provides a secure environment; trust is a foundational value of its proposition
- This would negatively impact adoption of Konflux, be it by clients or by the open-source community at large
